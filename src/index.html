DESTINATION

We’re heading back to the beautiful Isle of Islay, where we got engaged. Islay is a special place for both families, Kirsty’s dad grew up on Islay and  Ben’s mum used to holiday there.

We couldn’t help fall in love with Islay House right off the bat, it’s one of Scotland’s grandest and most historic country house hotels, located at the centre of the Queen of the Hebrides, Islay.

The house is set in 28 acres of gardens and protected woodland overlooking Loch Indaal, and has played host to many distinguished guests from Prime Ministers to Queen Elizabeth II. Now we are thrilled it will host the  Dunn Kelly Wedding!

TRAVEL AND TRANSPORT


Direct flights from Glasgow to Islay Glenegedale. Check out Logan Air.

Browse on Skyscanner

The ferry departs from Kennacraig, West Loch Tarbert. The two routes to Islay are to Port Ellen or Port Askaig. Book with Caledonian MacBrayne.

Fancy a road trip? The drive to Kennacraig Ferry Terminal from Manchester is approx. 6 hours or 9 hours from London.

Transport is limited on the island so hiring a car would make it easier to get around! Have a look at Islay Car Hire or DN MacKenzie.

THE WEDDING


The island's principal town is Bowmore where the distinctive round Kilarrow Parish Church (The round church) is perched at the top of the main street overlooking the harbour. We will begin our wedding ceremony here, another place close to our hearts as Kirsty’s late grandfather was an elder and her dad was christened at this very church.

The ceremony will kick off at 1:00pm, grab yourself a decent seat from 12:30pm.

After the ceremony, we will be heading to Islay House to begin the celebrations. It is just a short drive from the church, but for those of you without a car there will be a coach waiting to take you to Islay House.

FAQs

Q: Should I make a holiday of it?

A: Absolutely yes! We would love to see as many of you at the pub the night before the big day. We will be sticking around for a week, if the weather is good we’ll be planning a day at the beach, BBQ, a round of golf at the world famous Machrie links, afternoon tea and distillery tours (gin & whisky).

Q: Are children invited?

A: As much as we love the little ones, due to space at Islay House we won’t be able to accommodate children apart from family/wedding party. If you are bringing grandparents along for a holiday/babysitting duty, children are more than welcome to attend the Friday and other events. Or plan a nice weekend getaway and let your hair down!

Q: Is there a gift list?

A: Nope, you making it up to Islay is enough of a present! However if you feel compelled to give us one, a contribution to our honeymoon would be most appreciated.

Q: What is the weather likely to be like?

A: Unpredictable! August is one of the warmest and driest months of the year and we were very lucky last summer with 7 glorious days of sunshine! However, pack all weather gear as rain can come in off the Atlantic at any moment.

Q: What is the dress code?

A: Dress to impress! Kilts and tartan will be aplenty, it is the Scottish highlands afterall! We know you’ll all be looking fabulous!

Q: Do I need to learn how to ceilidh?

A: Don’t worry, the band will walk you through it all step by step! It’s just a bit of Scottish fun!

Q: Will there be pizza?

A: Without a doubt, it’s a Dunn Kelly wedding! The delicious Scozzese will be churning out wood-fired pizzas in the evening.

We can’t wait to share our big day with you!
